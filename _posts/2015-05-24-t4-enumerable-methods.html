What is Enumerable method?

An enumerable method is an object that may be enumerated. "Enumerated" means to count off the members of a set/collection/category one by one (usually in order, usually by name).

Enumerable methods can also be used to filter a collection of objects based on one or more selection criteria. One such method is group_by.

group_by groups the collection by result of the block. Returns a hash where the keys are the evaluated result from the block and the values are arrays of elements in the collection that correspond to the key. The syntax:
